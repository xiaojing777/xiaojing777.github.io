About

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate in Statistics. My research develops and applies statistical, machine learning, and deep learning methods for wearable device data, personalized mobile health monitoring, flexible copula modeling, treatment effect heterogeneity, and accelerated insurance underwriting.

Research Interests

  • Heterogeneous functional models for individualized effects and subgroup discovery on large-scale wearable data.
  • Evaluation and responsible deployment of risk models in high-stakes settings (e.g., accelerated underwriting).
  • Flexible non-exchangeable copulas capturing full-range tail dependence and asymmetry.
  • Representation learning pipelines for accelerometer data, including LLM-inspired approaches and interpretability probes.

Selected Working Papers / Research Projects

  • Generalized Heterogeneous Functional Model (GHFM) (2024) — personalized functional effects + subgroup identification; applied to UK Biobank; under review (arXiv: 2501.01135).
  • Flexible Non-exchangeable Bivariate Copulas (2025) — Pareto mixture construction; mono-vine structure; angular tail dependence; ready for journal submission.
  • Representation Learning for Wearable Accelerometer Data with LLMs (ongoing) — person-level embeddings; evaluation across downstream statistical tasks; time-masking interpretability experiments.
  • Heterogeneous Additive Functional Cox Models for Accelerated Underwriting (ongoing) — tensor-product B-splines; fusion + smoothness penalties; ADMM optimization; NHANES application.
  • Federated Data Augmentation via Inter-client Knowledge Transfer (ongoing) — privacy-preserving modality synthesis to improve inference accuracy on UK Biobank / All of Us / NHANES.
